Who we are

Social

The Indian Students Association (ISA) is one of the largest not for profit and culturally diverse associations at Arizona State University (ASU). All the students coming from India are proud members of this association. ISA offers its services to all students starting from prospective students of ASU to Alumni.

Culture

The Indian Students Association (ISA) is an organization established to provide guidance and assistance to the Indian students at Arizona State University. Ever since its inception, ISA has made students feel at home by actively organizing information sessions, cultural and recreational activities. ISA is a not for profit organization that is run primarily by students under the guidance of a faculty advisor- Drew Ross. We are involved in activities right form picking up new students and giving them temporary accommodation to organizing events which help us remember our roots.

Service

With the help of generous sponsors, continuing support from the current student members and guidance by its alumni, ISA hopes to keep helping the Indian Student community during their journey at Arizona State University amidst a fun environment filled with cultural and recreational activities

About Arizona State University

Arizona State University

Arizona State University (ASU) is one of the largest public research universities in United States under a single administration, with total student enrollment of 83,301 as of 2014 including 66,309 undergraduate and 15,751 graduate students.
ASU’s Tempe campus lies in the heart of Tempe, Arizona about eight miles (13 km) east of downtown Phoenix. The campus is approximately 642 acres (2.6 km2) in size and growing.

About Tempe

Tempe

The City of Tempe, Arizona is the state’s seventh largest city, is located in the center of the Valley of the Sun with a population of 164,268 (as of 2011). Phoenix, Scottsdale, Mesa, and Chandler border the City of Tempe. Access to Tempe is a snap with major highways and expressways serving it, making it the most accessible city in the metropolitan area. And, it is only 10 minutes from Sky Harbor International Airport with taxi service and public transportation available seven days a week.

Climate

The City of Tempe, Arizona offers more than 300 days an year of sunshine and annual rainfall of approximately 9.33 inches. Arizona being a desert has a hot climate with hot days and pleasant nights. May to September are the hottest months of the year, with some rainfall in the months of August, September and October. Temperature in the summers ranges from 100°F to 118°F (38°C to 48°C). The heat is dry with very little humidity. The nights and early mornings tend to get pleasant and the temperatures drop down to 90°F (32°C). The winters in Tempe are pleasant. The average high between the months of November and March being 65°F (18°C) and the average low being 42°F (5°C).

Transportation

Freeways make up the major transportation system for the Valley. The City of Tempe operates a free neighborhood circulator service called Orbit involving five free shuttle routes near Arizona State University that operate on a regular basis seven days a week. Valley Metro operates bus routes around Tempe and valley metro light rail system. Three other FLASH (Free Local Area Shuttle) circulate in northern Tempe around the University. ASU Provides free daily shuttle services to and from campuses around Tempe.

Around Tempe

A vibrant urban oasis in Metropolitan Phoenix, Tempe exudes its own personality. Refreshingly offbeat, Tempe’s a fun town that hosts family-friendly festivals, welcomes outdoor activities and is the proud home of Arizona State University. Tempe cares about community, stresses an active lifestyle and emphasizes a laid-back atmosphere.
